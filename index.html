What we found.
How to fix it.

What's Wrong
  • Gabit's teal and gold are nowhere on LinkedIn. Different fonts on every carousel, different styles on every static, no visual signature.
  • Static posts, carousels, and video thumbnails look like they came from three different people.
The Fix
  • One master template system: Poppins bold for headlines, teal accent for key phrases, dark background for video thumbnails, light for carousels.
  • Every post gets the Gabit teal-gold accent bar at the top or bottom, instant brand recall.
  • Podcast thumbnails: your face large, dark background, teal title card, bold contrasting text. No small text. No cluttered layouts.
  • Consistency compounds, after 30 days, people recognise your post before they read it.
What's Wrong
  • "A calm mind compounds quietly", no Gaurav Gupta fingerprint. Any wellness coach could post this.
  • Hooks are statements, not tensions. The most compelling insight is always buried. LinkedIn rewards the first line that demands a scroll.
The Fix
  • "Most health apps track your steps. Gabit tracks whether you'll live to 90.", that's your hook. Lead with the tension, not the resolution.
  • Anchor every post to your unfair advantage: the Zomato years, the Gabit data, the founder operator story.
What's Wrong
  • Captions read like they were written by AI, generic intro, no personal context, no story behind the clip. People can tell in 2 seconds and keep scrolling.
  • The caption never answers: what were you and Prakhar actually debating in this moment? That specific tension is what makes someone want to watch.
The Fix
  • Caption formula: open with your POV on the moment, "I disagreed with Prakhar on this one...", then the insight, then the watch. That's what gets the click.
  • Branded thumbnail: your face + guest + Gabit teal background. Every clip should scream Gabit before anyone reads a word.
What's Wrong
  • "Building Gabit" tells a visitor nothing. The bio is a product catalogue, not a vision.
  • Zomato and WEF are buried at the bottom, these are trust anchors, not footnotes.
The Fix
  • Try: "Adding healthy years to your life, not just tracking them. Founder, Gabit | Ex-Zomato | WEF Young Global Leader."
  • Lead with mission: "Most health tech tells you how you slept. We tell you how to live longer." Zomato + WEF in the first paragraph.
